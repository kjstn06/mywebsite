ADIDAS

History of Adidas

Adidas has a storied history that began in the early 20th century when Adolf Dassler started making sports shoes in his mother's laundry room after World War I. In 1924, he and his brother Rudolf founded the Dassler Brothers Shoe Factory, gaining fame when Jesse Owens wore their shoes at the 1936 Berlin Olympics. Following a split in 1949, Adolf rebranded his company as Adidas, derived from his name. The brand became known for its innovative designs, such as the iconic three-stripe logo, and gained further recognition with the 1970 FIFA World Cup ball. In the 1980s, Adidas embraced lifestyle and fashion, becoming a staple in hip-hop culture through collaborations with artists like Run-D.M.C. The company continued to expand in the 1990s and 2000s, acquiring Reebok and focusing on technology and sustainability. Today, Adidas remains a leader in sportswear and lifestyle apparel, committed to performance, innovation, and sustainability while maintaining strong ties to sports.

Best Sellers of Adidas

Adidas OG Samba

Whether you're taking a casual stroll or heading out for the evening, the adidas Samba OG shoes have you covered. This iconic silhouette has been kicking around for decades, transitioning from football pitches to city streets while picking up fans along the way. The smooth leather upper, suede T-toe and gum rubber outsole are timeless details that link the shoe to its sporty beginnings. But now, the Samba is all about laid-back style. Slip these on and you'll feel instantly comfortable yet effortlessly cool. A classic that's meant to stand the test of time, the Samba lets you walk to the beat of your own drum.

Adidas Gazelle

Once a training shoe, now a timeless icon, these adidas Gazelle shoes let you walk in the footsteps of history. This pair honours the beloved version from 1991 with a soft and supple suede upper and recognisable rubber outsole. Eye-catching colours lend modern style and pair well with casual outfits or sporty attire.

Adidas Forum Low 84

A favourite of basketball greats, platinum-selling musical artists and everyday style mavens, the adidas Forum is a symbol of greatness. Although they've moved on from the hardwood, these shoes carry on the Forum legacy with OG 1984 design elements like the hook-and-loop ankle strap and iconic X-detail. A premium leather upper and chunky outsole bring the look home for elite everyday style.
